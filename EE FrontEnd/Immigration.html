Immigration



DACA

President Trump moved in 2017 to phase out the Obama-era Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals program, which offers deportation relief and work permits to undocumented immigrants brought to the U.S. as children. Trump's decision — temporarily blocked by federal courts — left the status of nearly 700,000 "Dreamers" in limbo. Democrats criticized the move and will likely strike back with their own proposals to provide a pathway to citizenship for DACA recipients and a broader pool of undocumented immigrants.

The Wall

The border wall is Trump's signature issue — and Democrats are taking stands against it. Most candidates oppose Trump's attempt to get billions to build more wall. Some have even said they're open to tearing down some existing barriers. Another faction is against the wall, but would consider more funding as part of a broader immigration deal.
