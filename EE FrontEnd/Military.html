Military



Defense Spending

Donald Trump won the White House pledging a "great rebuilding of the armed forces" after years of across-the-board budget cuts and growing signs of strain on the military. A two-year, bipartisan budget deal reached with Congress in 2018 increased the Pentagon's coffers to as high as $716 billion. In early 2019, the Trump administration doubled down by proposing a new $750 billion annual budget for the next fiscal year — the largest ever — even as the president reportedly had second thoughts on what he called the Pentagon's "crazy" spending spree. The new request is being considered by Congress, where Democrats now control the House and are less inclined to go all in. Some Democratic leaders accuse the Trump administration of crying wolf when it contends it needs every penny -- especially since it was able to redirect some $2.5 billion from previously approved Defense Department funds to pay for a border barrier.
