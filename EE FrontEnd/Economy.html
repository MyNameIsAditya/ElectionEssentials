Affordable Housing

The rising cost of housing has led nearly a third of American households to spend more than the recommended 30 percent of their income on a mortgage or rent. Limited supply means rents in cities are skyrocketing, while rampant student debt is preventing many young people from buying homes.

Minimum Wage

Congress hasn't legislated a national minimum-wage increase since 2007 (when it hiked the hourly minimum to $7.25 effective July 2009), so Democrats and even some Republicans agree a raise is overdue. Candidate Donald Trump took multiple contradictory positions on the issue in 2016, eventually endorsing a $10 minimum, but as president he let it drop. Trump's National Economic Council director, Larry Kudlow, said in November that the very existence of a federal minimum wage was a "terrible idea." Labor Secretary Alexander Acosta told Congress in May 2019 that he opposes any hike to the federal minimum wage.

Reforming Socioeconomic Inequality

The U.S. economy is humming along, but a growing share of the benefits are going to those already at the top. The disconnect is a significant concern for voters, who appear to be widely supportive of raising taxes on the wealthy, for example. Democratic presidential candidates are offering a growing list of proposals to tackle the issue, including dramatic steps to redistribute the nation's wealth. Some are offering tax hikes on the wealthy while others propose tax cuts for the middle class. Many support raising the minimum wage, but some are going further by proposing universal basic income and a federal jobs guarantee.

Candidate Candidate Image Stance Political Party
Andrew Yang Pay Americans a universal basic income. Among 2020 presidential candidates, universal basic income is the most sweeping proposed solution for addressing income inequality. It would require the government to make regular payments to all U.S. citizens, regardless of whether or not they're working, no strings attached. "UBI" is the central policy proposal of Andrew Yang, who argues that it will help Americans at risk of losing their jobs because of technological advances. Democratic
Cory Booker Raise Taxes on the wealthy, create new social programs. Sen. Cory Booker has proposed creating "opportunity accounts" containing $1,000 for every American child at birth. The government would pay into the accounts throughout the child's life and make the money available at age 18 for homes and higher education. Booker would fund the accounts by raising estate and capital gains taxes. Democratic
